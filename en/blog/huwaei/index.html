Huawei

  • By Kirubel Kibru
  • at February 22, 2017
To buy a smart phones there are certain criteria's that should be met before you decide to buy I have listed all below.

1.Performance when your buying a phone you have to be sure that your phone is equipped with the necessary specs to handle what you need it to do. 

2.Battery just being powerful isn't enough it should also be able to do anything you want for as long as possible. 

3.Build Quality this very important cause the material enclosing your battery and processing system should be  durable even,if dropped able it should be able to withstand the damage. Keep it running smoothly and maintain its temperature very well.

4.Money I understand people would have bought a phone with all those but its the amount of money there willing to spend. Therefore, buying something worth what your spending is the main objective.







So for today will be looking at the Huawei P9 made by a Chinese company with the cooperation of leica. The aim of this company is to hit the mid priced smart phone market making smart phones accessible to everyone.That is why Huawie has set out to change the market by influencing people to also consider their products since there already well established companies such as Samsung, Apple and Nokia. They presented the Huawei P9 on April 2016 available with 3GB Ram/32GB storage and also 4GB ram/64GB storage.Equipped with a powerful Octa-core (4x2.5Ghz Cortex-A72 & 4x1.8Ghz Cortex -A53) it could handle anything you throw at it with flying colors.

Looking onto the build quality I didn't like it too much. They should have used more metal it would have been more durable, but nevertheless it looks great with a semi glass finish on the top with metal on the side.Coming to the display it has a 1080x1920 beautiful display playing 1080 videos at 60fps,and 720 at 120fps(frames per second).

This isn't to bad but sadly doesn't support 4K (please refer to our other articles if your not familiar with this display).The camera 13MP with 1080p recording possible,and if your looking for high quality photos I wouldn't recommend you to buy it.A battery with 3000mAh Li-lon (sealed) this would do you good for a day if you run simple applications,but if your pushing it to the limit expect at least a 5hr long battery.Fast charging is also possible getting 45% in just 30minutes.It also has a finger print senser , a microsd slot upgradable to (256 GB) and two sims slots (Supporting both 4G,and 3G connection).

It's quite light because of its finishing touch is plastic. In overall this a good phone for the average consumer it's cheap if your not willing to spend that much just by comprising a bit on the build quality it will a great investment.This could also be a good start up phone for our fellow Africans this will get them more acquainted with smart phones.


So lets scale it up 1=>bad, 2=>good, 3=>V.good,and 4=>Excellent.

1.Performance=3
2.Battery=2
3.Build=2
4.Money=4 approximately about 260$ to 290$
